# P07 — “What does a cell do when it suddenly runs out of amino acids?”

*Case 4.2 · theme: stringent response / ppGpp · operationalizations reused from the human-pilot rep-0 hypotheses.*

> **Setup.** The Socratic Council and the Cellarium agent each operationalized the same question. ## The two operationalizations

### Socratic Council

- **Claim (H1):** When E. coli suddenly runs out of amino acids, the cell activates the stringent response: ppGpp concentration rises sharply (≥3-fold within minutes), charged tRNA fraction drops, and growth rate falls, mediated by RelA sensing uncharged tRNA at the ribosome.
- **H0:** Sudden amino-acid depletion does not trigger a ppGpp-mediated stringent response; ppGpp, charged-tRNA fraction, and growth rate remain near basal levels.
- **Predicted effect:** ppGpp concentration rises ≥3-fold (from ~10 µM basal to ≥30 µM) within 300 s of amino-acid removal; fraction_trna_charged drops ≥50% (from ~0.9 to ≤0.45); growth_rate falls ≥80% (from ~0.5 to ≤0.1 1/h) within 600 s.
- **Operationalize** "stringent response activation" -> `ppgpp_conc` (ppGpp concentration (µM) sampled every 60 s after amino-acid removal; response = fold-change relative to pre-shift baseline)
- **Operationalize** "amino-acid starvation" -> `fraction_trna_charged` (fraction of tRNA aminoacylated; starvation indicated by drop below 0.5 within 300 s of shift)
- **Operationalize** "growth arrest" -> `growth_rate` (instantaneous specific growth rate (1/h) post-shift; arrest = drop to ≤20% of pre-shift value)
- **Operationalize** "RelA-mediated synthesis" -> `rela_conc` (RelA protein concentration (µM); causal test via gene_knockout of relA)
- **Falsifier:** on `ppgpp_conc` compare amino_acid_shift/aa_removal vs wildtype/basal; Welch t-test comparing mean ppGpp concentration in the 300–600 s window post-shift (target) vs. steady-state basal (reference); reject H1 if |t| < 2 OR if fold-change < 2.0; refuted if ppGpp concentration remains within 2-fold of basal (≤20 µM) after amino-acid removal, indistinguishable from reference (|t| < 2)
- **Rival:** Rival 1 (passive dilution): Growth arrest is a passive consequence of substrate limitation, not an active regulatory program; ppGpp does not rise because RelA is not activated. -> distinguished by: In a relA knockout (gene_knockout relA / aa_removal), ppGpp remains near zero and growth rate still drops, but charged-tRNA fraction falls identically to wildtype, showing the growth phenotype persists without ppGpp signaling.
- **Rival:** Rival 2 (translational jamming): Uncharged tRNA causes ribosome stalling and growth arrest independently of ppGpp; the ppGpp rise is epiphenomenal. -> distinguished by: Clamping ppGpp to basal levels (ppgpp_conc clamp at 1.0× / aa_removal) prevents growth-rate recovery but does NOT prevent the initial drop in growth_rate or charged-tRNA fraction, indicating ppGpp modulates adaptation but is not required for the immediate arrest.
- **Auxiliary assumption:** The simulator's RelA module is sensitive to charged-tRNA fraction and synthesizes ppGpp accordingly.
- **Auxiliary assumption:** The amino_acid_shift perturbation instantaneously depletes all supplemented amino acids at the specified timeline point.
- **Auxiliary assumption:** Basal condition provides sufficient amino acids initially; their removal is the sole perturbation.
- **Auxiliary assumption:** The 300–600 s post-shift window captures the acute stringent-response phase before secondary adaptations dominate.
- **Auxiliary assumption:** No other starvation signal (carbon, nitrogen, phosphate) is triggered by amino-acid removal alone.
- **Auxiliary assumption:** The model does not explicitly track individual amino-acid species; 'amino_acid_shift' is a composite toggle.
- **Auxiliary assumption:** Stochastic noise across seeds is smaller than the predicted 3-fold ppGpp increase (replicate variation ~20% CV).

## Phase 1 — Opening briefs (blind; each argues against *any* other operationalization)

### Socratic Council — counsel's brief

#### Thesis

This operationalization is correct because it translates "what a cell does" into a mechanistically testable causal chain—ppGpp synthesis by RelA in response to uncharged tRNA, leading to growth arrest—using three complementary observables that together constitute the stringent response, and discriminates this active regulatory program from passive dilution and translational-jamming alternatives.

---

#### Why this operationalization is faithful to the question

The raw question asks what a cell **does** when starved of amino acids, implying an active, detectable response rather than mere cessation.

**Mapping of constructs to observables:**

- **"runs out of amino acids"** → `fraction_trna_charged` dropping below 0.5 within 300 s after `amino_acid_shift` removes supplemented amino acids. This directly measures the depletion's molecular consequence: uncharged tRNA accumulation.
- **"suddenly"** → Timeline design with instantaneous shift at t=1200 s; sampling every 60 s captures acute dynamics (300–600 s window), not slow adaptation.
- **"what the cell does"** → Three coordinated observables:
  1. **`ppgpp_conc`** rising ≥3-fold: the alarmone synthesis is the canonical *action* E. coli takes during amino-acid starvation.
  2. **`growth_rate`** falling ≥80%: the functional outcome—growth arrest—demonstrates the response's phenotypic impact.
  3. **`rela_conc`** as the mechanistic linchpin, tested via `gene_knockout relA`, ensuring the response is not merely correlative but causally mediated by the known sensor.

**Nothing essential is dropped:**  
The question's implicit scope—*bacterial stress response*—is respected by focusing on the stringent response, E. coli's primary amino-acid starvation program, rather than secondary adaptations (e.g., proteolysis, autophagy analogs). The 300–600 s window isolates the acute regulatory decision before metabolic remodeling confounds interpretation.

**Nothing is smuggled in:**  
We do not assume *which* genes are transcriptionally reprogrammed or *how many* ribosomes stall—only that ppGpp rises, tRNA charging falls, and growth slows. The operationalization measures the response's **existence and mechanism**, not its downstream complexity.

---

#### Why it is decisive

**Falsifiability:**  
H₁ makes three quantitative, time-resolved predictions (≥3-fold ppGpp rise, ≥50% tRNA-charging drop, ≥80% growth-rate fall) within specified windows. The decision rule—Welch *t* ≥ 2 **and** fold-change ≥ 2.0 on `ppgpp_conc` at 300–600 s—ensures replicable statistical power (10 seeds, ~20% CV noise) while avoiding false positives from mere noise spikes. H₀ is rejected if ppGpp remains within 2-fold of basal, a clear threshold.

**Discriminating power against rivals:**

1. **Rival 1 (passive dilution):**  
   The `gene_knockout relA / aa_removal` design tests whether growth arrest requires ppGpp signaling. If ppGpp stays near zero in ΔrelA yet growth still drops, the rival (substrate limitation alone causes arrest) is supported. If growth arrest is *attenuated* in ΔrelA, H₁ (active ppGpp-mediated program) wins. The observables distinguish correlation from causation.

2. **Rival 2 (translational jamming):**  
   The `ppgpp_conc clamp 1.0× / aa_removal` design asks: does clamping ppGpp at basal prevent growth arrest? If arrest still occurs identically, ppGpp is epiphenomenal (rival wins). If the *kinetics* differ (e.g., delayed recovery, altered tRNA-charging dynamics), H₁'s claim that ppGpp mediates the regulatory *response* (not just the stall) is vindicated. The clamp isolates ppGpp's causal role from uncharged-tRNA's direct ribosomal effects.

**Feasibility on this instrument:**  
All four designs—wildtype basal, `amino_acid_shift`, `gene_knockout relA`, `ppgpp_conc clamp`—are explicitly listed perturbations. The three channels (`ppgpp_conc`, `fraction_trna_charged`, `growth_rate`) are available dials. The timeline syntax (`0 basal+amino_acids, 1200 basal-amino_acids`) fits the schema. The 60 s sampling matches the instrument's temporal resolution (1200 s runs, 3 generations ≈ 90 min at basal growth). The designs are orthogonal (knockout tests mechanism, clamp tests necessity) and executable within stated constraints.

#### Why any other operationalization is weaker

**Alternative 1: Broader observable set (e.g., track all 20 amino-acid species individually).**  
*Failure mode:* The instrument's `amino_acid_shift` is a composite toggle, not species-specific (per auxiliary assumptions). Measuring individual amino acids requires `species_kinds = metabolite` granularity not guaranteed by dial labels. Even if accessible, 20 separate time-courses introduce multiple-comparisons noise without added discriminatory power—the *aggregate* tRNA-charging fraction already integrates amino-acid availability. Faithfulness preserved, falsifiability weakened (harder to meet statistical threshold), feasibility uncertain.

**Alternative 2: Narrower observable (ppGpp alone, ignoring tRNA charging and growth).**  
*Failure mode:* Measuring only `ppgpp_conc` rise does not confirm amino-acid depletion *occurred* (maybe the shift failed) or that the response was *functional* (ppGpp could spike without growth impact in a broken model). Without `fraction_trna_charged`, we lack the *stimulus* confirmation; without `growth_rate`, we lack the *outcome* confirmation. This operationalization is less faithful (drops the "what the cell does" phenotype) and less falsifiable (rivals could argue ppGpp rose spuriously, not causally).

**Alternative 3: Different statistic (e.g., area-under-curve of ppGpp over 0–1200 s instead of fold-change at 300–600 s).**

Growth rate is the integrative phenotype the question implicitly targets.

**Alternative 5: Protein/RNA mass ratios instead of tRNA charging.**

Without the knockout and clamp designs, the operationalization is correlative, not mechanistic—less decisive. The question "what does a cell *do*" implies agency (regulatory program), which requires causal disambiguation.

### Cellarium agent — counsel's brief

#### Thesis

This operationalization is correct because it translates "what a cell does" into the four canonical, directly observable hallmarks of the stringent response cascade—charged tRNA fraction, ppGpp accumulation, growth arrest, and ribosome reallocation—measured on a common timescale that captures acute regulatory response while discriminating active signaling from passive starvation.

#### Why this operationalization is faithful to the question

**Mapping constructs to observables:**

- **"runs out of amino acids"** → `fraction_trna_charged` dropping ≥40% confirms depletion at the molecular level where it matters (aminoacyl-tRNA synthetase substrate exhaustion), not merely external concentration.
- **"suddenly"** → `timeline: 0 basal, 10 basal_minus_amino_acids` enforces an abrupt step-function removal, and the 10-minute window isolates acute response from slower adaptive transcription.

**Nothing essential is dropped:** The question asks what happens, not why; the operationalization measures the observable consequence (ppGpp spike, growth halt) without requiring mechanistic simulation of every intermediate enzyme. **Falsifiability:**  
H₀ states observables remain near baseline; H₁ specifies quantitative thresholds (≥3-fold ppGpp, ≥50% growth drop, ≥40% tRNA uncharged). If amino acid removal uniquely produces the largest `ppgpp_conc` spike *and* the steepest `fraction_trna_charged` drop, specificity is demonstrated. #### Why any other operationalization is weaker

**Alternative 1: Measure only `growth_rate` (narrower).**  
*Failure mode:* Growth could drop for many reasons (substrate depletion, osmotic shock, mechanical damage). Without `ppgpp_conc` or `fraction_trna_charged`, you cannot distinguish active stringent response from passive metabolic collapse. A ΔrelA strain would show identical growth arrest under true starvation (no ATP for anabolism) but no ppGpp spike; this alternative cannot discriminate. It is less falsifiable because it conflates multiple causal pathways.

**Alternative 2: Measure `rela_conc` (protein level) instead of `ppgpp_conc` (product).**  
*Failure mode:* RelA activation is post-translational (binding to ribosome-tRNA complex). `rela_conc` may not change on the 10-min timescale; you would see no effect even if RelA is frantically synthesizing ppGpp. The product (`ppgpp_conc`) is the actual effector and the canonical stringent-response marker. Measuring the enzyme is mechanistically one step removed and empirically insensitive.

**Alternative 3: Single timepoint at 60 min (broader window).**  
*Failure mode:* By 60 min, transcriptional reprogramming, amino acid biosynthesis pathway upregulation, and possibly recovery dynamics confound the acute response. The question asks what the cell *does* in response to sudden depletion, not what steady-state it eventually reaches. A late timepoint mixes primary (ppGpp signaling) and secondary (biosynthetic adaptation) responses, reducing causal clarity. The 10-min snapshot isolates the regulatory trigger.

**Alternative 4: Use `protein_mass` or `rna_mass` as readouts (different observable).**  
*Failure mode:* These are slow integrals of synthesis rates. Protein/RNA mass changes lag behind regulatory signals (ppGpp, tRNA charging). At 10 min, mass may barely budge even if synthesis rates have plummeted. `growth_rate` is the instantaneous derivative and responds immediately; `ribosome_conc` is a dynamic pool. The alternative observables are insensitive on the decisive timescale.

**Alternative 5: Operationalize "runs out" as external amino acid concentration = 0 without measuring `fraction_trna_charged`.**  
*Failure mode:* External depletion does not guarantee intracellular exhaustion. Cells have amino acid pools; synthetases have Kₘ values. You need `fraction_trna_charged` to confirm starvation at the mechanistic locus (ribosome A-site). Without it, a null result could mean "the simulator's internal pool buffered the external drop," not "the cell didn't respond." The operationalization must close the loop from perturbation to molecular consequence.

**Alternative 6: Use a different statistic (e.g., median fold-change, no t-test).**  
*Failure mode:* Median fold-change ignores replicate variance; you cannot distinguish a noisy 2-fold shift from a tight 2-fold shift. The Welch t-test (|t|≥2) incorporates both effect size *and* consistency across seeds, increasing confidence that the result is not stochastic artifact. The alternative is less rigorous about separating signal from intrinsic noise.

#### Concessions

**Debatable choice 1:** The 10-min sampling window is a compromise. Faster (5 min) might catch the ppGpp spike earlier but risk under-sampling if RelA activation kinetics are slow in the simulator; slower (20 min) risks conflating acute and adaptive responses. The auxiliary assumption that "10 min captures acute response before transcriptional adaptation dominates" is empirically grounded but not guaranteed in this simulator's parameter regime.

**Debatable choice 2:** `fraction_trna_charged` averages across all tRNA species. If the `amino_acid_shift` removes only one amino acid (e.g., histidine), the *global* tRNA charging fraction may drop modestly while histidyl-tRNA crashes. The operationalization's sensitivity depends on whether the simulator's `amino_acid_shift` removes *all* amino acids (as the raw question implies) or a subset. The claim assumes complete removal or at least removal of a rate-limiting subset.

**Vulnerability 1:** The `relA` knockout rival test assumes the simulator mechanistically models RelA-dependent ppGpp synthesis. If RelA is hard-coded or ppGpp dynamics are phenomenological, the knockout may show no effect even if the biology is correct. This is a feasibility gamble; we trust the instrument's gene knockout machinery but cannot verify it pre-test.

**Vulnerability 2:** The predicted magnitudes (3-fold ppGpp, 50% growth drop) are literature-inspired but the simulator's parameter fit may differ. If the simulator's basal ppGpp is 50 µM (not 10 µM), a 3-fold rise to 150 µM may still occur but the absolute scale is wrong. The decision rule uses fold-change to guard against this, but if the simulator's dynamic range is compressed, the threshold (≥3-fold) may be too stringent. The operationalization is vulnerable to simulator-specific calibration mismatches.

**Honest limitation:** The operationalization does not measure individual amino acid biosynthesis pathway fluxes (e.g., `reaction_flux` for IlvE, TrpA). It infers resource reallocation from ribosome drawdown but does not directly confirm upregulation of biosynthetic operons. A more complete operationalization would include mRNA or flux channels for amino acid pathways, but that would require knowing *which* pathways the simulator models, and the instrument spec does not enumerate species-level queries. The current choice prioritizes generality and instrument-agnostic feasibility over mechanistic completeness.
