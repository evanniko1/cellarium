*Case 1.1 · theme: intrinsic vs extrinsic noise · operationalizations reused from the human-pilot rep-0 hypotheses.*

Each then argued (blind) for its own operationalization against *any* alternative, read the other's brief, and returned an honest verdict. ## The two operationalizations

### Socratic Council

- **Claim (H1):** Genetically identical E. coli cells exhibit cell-to-cell variation in gene expression levels due to intrinsic stochastic noise, such that the coefficient of variation (CV) of protein abundance across isogenic cells under identical conditions is ≥0.15 for at least 50% of genes.
- **H0:** Genetically identical cells under identical conditions express each gene at the same level (CV ≈ 0, within numerical precision); any observed variation is negligible.
- **Predicted effect:** CV ≥ 0.15 for ≥50% of assayed genes. Predicted distribution: bimodal or right-skewed, with low-abundance genes (mean < 100 copies) showing CV ~0.2–0.5, and high-abundance genes showing CV ~0.1–0.2. After normalization by protein_mass, CV remains ≥0.15 for ≥50% of genes (indicating intrinsic noise dominates).
- **Operationalize** "gene expression level" -> `protein[GENE_ID]` (steady-state protein copy number (counts) for each gene, sampled across seeds at generation 10)
- **Operationalize** "cell-to-cell variation" -> `coefficient of variation (CV = std / mean) of protein[GENE_ID] across seeds` (CV computed over 50 independent stochastic replicates (seeds) for each gene)

- **Auxiliary assumption:** Generation 10 is sufficient for the system to reach a steady-state distribution of protein levels.
- **Auxiliary assumption:** The model computes per-gene protein counts (protein[GENE_ID] for each gene) and these are accessible as named species.

## Phase 1 — Opening briefs (blind; each argues against *any* other operationalization)

### Socratic Council — counsel's brief

#### Thesis
This operationalization is correct because it directly measures protein-level variance across isogenic replicates—the only empirical signature of stochastic gene expression—using the instrument's native stochasticity mechanism (seeds) and discriminates intrinsic from extrinsic noise through protein_mass normalization, thereby capturing the biological essence of "same gene, same level" as a distributional claim rather than a point estimate.

#### Why this operationalization is faithful to the question

**"Genetically identical cells"** → `wildtype/basal` with varying `seeds`  
The instrument generates isogenic populations by fixing genome and condition; `seeds` vary only the stochastic realization. This is the computational analogue of clonal bacteria in microfluidic chambers.

**"Same gene"** → `protein[GENE_ID]` for 20 pre-specified loci  
The instrument exposes per-gene protein counts as named species. Selecting 20 genes (rpoB, rpoC, rpoD, rpsA, rpsB, rplA, rplB, tufA, tufB, groEL, dnaK, lacZ, acrB, ompF, malE, araC, crp, fis, hns, lrp) spanning housekeeping, regulatory, and inducible functions ensures the test is not confined to atypical edge-cases but samples the expression-level distribution (low/medium/high abundance).

**"Same level"** → CV of `protein[GENE_ID]` across seeds  
The null hypothesis interprets "same level" as deterministic identity (CV ≈ 0). The alternative interprets it as empirical variance (CV ≥ 0.15), consistent with single-cell measurements. The question is inherently about whether a *distribution* collapses to a point or retains spread; CV is the natural statistic for comparing spread relative to mean.

**"Express"** → steady-state protein copy number at generation 10  
Gene expression level operationalizes to the downstream protein product (the functional output), not mRNA alone. Generation 10 ensures transient effects from initialization have dissipated and the system reflects steady-state stochastic equilibrium.

**Extrinsic vs. intrinsic discrimination** → normalization by `protein_mass`  
The raw question is ambiguous about whether "same level" means absolute counts or relative expression. Normalizing by total protein mass (a proxy for cell size/global state) isolates gene-specific stochasticity from correlated fluctuations in ribosome number, growth rate, or division timing. This directly addresses whether variation is *intrinsic to the gene* or a consequence of whole-cell extrinsic variation.

**No smuggled assumptions:**  
The operationalization does not presuppose the *magnitude* of noise (the threshold 0.15 is stated as a falsifiable claim, not assumed), nor does it select genes post-hoc. The 20-gene panel is fixed a priori. The use of CV (rather than variance) prevents confounding abundance with noisiness.

#### Why it is decisive

**Falsifiability:**  
H₀ ("CV ≈ 0") is rejected if ≥10 of 20 genes show CV ≥ 0.15. This is a one-sample binomial test (proportion > 0.5, α = 0.05), which has exact Type I error control. The null is *falsifiable* because it predicts a specific outcome (near-zero CV for all genes) that the measurement can directly contradict.

**Discriminating power against Rival 1 (extrinsic noise):**  
If variation were purely extrinsic (e.g., cell-size differences driving proportional changes in all proteins), then `protein[GENE_ID]/protein_mass` would collapse to near-constant values (CV_norm ≤ 0.05 for >80% of genes). The operationalization explicitly computes both CV_raw and CV_norm. If CV_norm remains ≥0.15 for ≥50% of genes, this *falsifies* the extrinsic-only hypothesis and supports intrinsic noise. This is a within-design discrimination: the same data adjudicates two rival mechanisms.

**Discriminating power against Rival 2 (deterministic chaos):**  
A deterministic chaotic system would produce identical trajectories for identical seeds. The operationalization uses 50 *distinct* seeds; any CV > 0 across these seeds confirms stochastic noise is implemented in the simulator. This is an auxiliary check (ensuring the instrument matches the assumption), but it is operationally testable: run one gene with 50 seeds vs. the same seed repeated 50 times and verify CV > 0 in the former, CV = 0 in the latter.

**Feasibility:**  
The design requires one perturbation (`wildtype`), one condition (`basal`), 50 seeds, 10 generations, and 20 genes × 2 channels (`protein[GENE_ID]`, `protein_mass`). The instrument schema explicitly supports all these elements. The decision rule is a simple binomial proportion test with no ambiguous cutoffs (the 0.15 threshold is justified by single-cell literature, but the test outcome is insensitive to moderate threshold shifts—CV distributions are typically bimodal or right-skewed, so the 50th percentile is robust). No complex timeline manipulations, no unverified gene knockouts, no reliance on reaction_flux or metabolite species whose simulation fidelity is unknown.

#### Why any other operationalization is weaker

**Alternative 1: Measure mRNA instead of protein (`mrna[GENE_ID]`)**  
*Weakness:* mRNA is upstream of the functional output. The question asks about "gene expression level," which biologically refers to the gene's contribution to cellular phenotype—proteins, not transcripts. mRNA noise can be buffered or amplified by translational stochasticity and protein degradation. Operationalizing to mRNA alone would miss post-transcriptional sources of variation (e.g., ribosome binding variability, which is intrinsic noise). Moreover, if CV_mrna were low but CV_protein high, the mRNA operationalization would falsely reject H₁, failing to capture the biological phenomenon the question targets.

**Alternative 2: Use variance instead of CV**  
*Weakness:* Variance scales with mean², confounding abundance with noisiness. A high-abundance housekeeping gene (e.g., rpoB, mean ~1000 copies) could have variance = 10,000 (SD = 100, CV = 0.1) while a low-abundance regulator (mean ~10 copies) has variance = 4 (SD = 2, CV = 0.2). Ranking by variance would spuriously flag high-abundance genes as "variable" even if their *relative* spread is modest. CV is scale-invariant and biologically interpretable (Poisson noise yields CV ∝ 1/√mean; super-Poissonian noise yields CV > 1/√mean). The question asks if cells express "the same gene" at "the same level"—this is a relative comparison within a gene, not across genes, so CV is the faithful statistic.

**Alternative 3: Test all ~1000 genes rather than a pre-specified 20**  
*Weakness:* This introduces multiple-testing burden (requiring Bonferroni or FDR correction, which inflates the false-negative rate) and risks post-hoc selection bias (cherry-picking genes that show noise). More critically, the instrument may not expose `protein[GENE_ID]` for *all* genes (the schema states "species_kinds" include "protein" but does not guarantee per-gene accessibility for the full genome). The 20-gene panel is chosen a priori to span abundance ranges and functional classes, providing a representative sample without assuming exhaustive coverage. If the test were run genome-wide and only 10% of genes showed CV ≥ 0.15, it would still support H₁ as written (majority of genes)—but if those 10% were low-abundance outliers, the claim would be less convincing. The 20-gene approach avoids this by ensuring diversity in the tested set.

**Alternative 4: Use a single seed with many generations (temporal variance)**  
*Weakness:* Temporal variance *within* a lineage conflates intrinsic noise with dynamical fluctuations (e.g., cell-cycle periodicity, metabolic oscillations). The question asks whether "cells" (plural, implying a population) express genes at the same level. A single lineage is one realization of the stochastic process; variance over time in that lineage does not address whether *different cells* are equivalent. The correct test is variance *across* isogenic cells at the *same* timepoint (steady state), which is what seeds provide. Temporal variance would also fail to distinguish extrinsic from intrinsic noise (a single cell experiences both).

**Alternative 5: Threshold at CV ≥ 0.05 or CV ≥ 0.30 instead of 0.15**  
*Weakness:* CV ≥ 0.05 is too permissive—it would classify near-deterministic genes (e.g., those with Poisson-level fluctuations, CV = 1/√mean ≈ 0.03 for mean = 1000) as "variable," reducing discriminating power against H₀. CV ≥ 0.30 is too stringent—it would miss genes with moderate but biologically significant noise (e.g., bursty low-abundance regulators with CV ≈ 0.2). The 0.15 threshold is grounded in experimental single-cell studies (Elowitz 2002, Taniguchi 2010) showing typical CV for intrinsic noise is ~0.1–0.3; the median of this range provides a robust, pre-registered cutoff that is neither trivially permissive nor unrealistically stringent.

**Alternative 6: Compare wildtype/basal at generation 10 vs. generation 20 (temporal reproducibility)**  
*Weakness:* This tests whether the *system* reaches a stationary distribution, not whether *cells* are identical. If CV at generation 10 and generation 20 are both 0.15, this confirms steady state but does not adjudicate H₁ vs. H₀. The question is about cell-to-cell variation, not temporal stability. The operationalization already assumes generation 10 is sufficient for steady state (stated in auxiliary assumptions); testing this assumption is orthogonal to the hypothesis.

#### Concessions

**Threshold arbitrariness:**  
The 0.15 CV cutoff, while empirically motivated, is a convention. Genes with CV = 0.14 would be classified as non-variable, potentially underestimating the prevalence of noise. A sensitivity analysis (re-running the test at CV = 0.10, 0.15, 0.20) would strengthen the claim, but is not included in the operationalization. The binomial test is robust to moderate threshold shifts, but the boundary is inherently somewhat arbitrary.

### Cellarium agent — counsel's brief

#### Thesis
This operationalization is correct because it directly measures **distributional heterogeneity in protein abundance across isogenic replicates**, which is the precise biological phenomenon the question asks about, while using coefficient of variation as a statistically principled, scale-invariant metric that discriminates intrinsic stochastic noise from both measurement artifact and rival biological mechanisms.

---

#### Why this operationalization is faithful to the question

**Mapping of constructs:**

1. **"Genetically identical cells"** → `isogenic stochastic replicates (seeds ≥30) of wildtype/basal design`  
   Each seed represents one cell with identical genome and environment; the instrument's seed parameter explicitly varies only intrinsic stochastic trajectories, not genotype or conditions.

2. **"Same gene"** → `named protein species readout`  
   The instrument provides protein copy number per species; selecting the same protein species across seeds means measuring the same gene's expression product.

3. **"Expression level"** → `protein copy number per cell`  
   Protein abundance is the functional readout of gene expression (mRNA is intermediate; protein is what acts). The instrument reports absolute copy number per cell at single-cell resolution.

4. **"Express the same ... at the same level"** → `CV ≤ 0.05 (H0) vs CV > 0.2 (H1)`  
   "Same level" requires low cell-to-cell variation; CV (coefficient of variation = σ/μ) is the standard statistical measure of relative dispersion, scale-invariant across genes with different mean abundances. The threshold 0.05 represents negligible variation (~5% noise, near measurement error); 0.2 represents substantial biological heterogeneity.

**Nothing essential is dropped:**  
- The question is about **population heterogeneity among identical cells**, which is precisely what distributing measurements across seeds captures.  
- Using protein copy number (not mRNA, not flux) reflects the biological reality that protein levels determine phenotype.  

**Nothing is smuggled in:**  
- The claim does not presuppose a *mechanism* (intrinsic noise vs extrinsic noise vs cell-cycle effects) — it only operationalizes *what to measure* (protein CV). The mechanistic hypothesis (intrinsic stochasticity) is tested explicitly via rivals that discriminate mechanisms.

---

#### Why it is decisive

**Falsifiability:**  
The decision rule is binary and quantitative:  
- Compute CV for ≥10 genes across ≥30 seeds at steady state.  
- Reject H1 if <50% of genes show CV > 0.2 **or** if median CV ≤ 0.1.  

This creates a clear bright line. A world where cells express genes uniformly (H0) would yield median CV ≤ 0.1 and most genes with CV ≤ 0.05. A world with substantial intrinsic noise (H1) would yield median CV ≈ 0.25 and >50% of genes with CV > 0.2.

**Discriminating power against rivals:**

1. **Rival: Extrinsic noise (global cellular state fluctuations)**  
   - **Distinguishing test:** Measure pairwise Pearson correlation of protein copy numbers for unrelated genes across seeds.  
   - **Discrimination:** Extrinsic noise (shared ribosome/resource fluctuations) couples expression of all genes → high correlations (r > 0.7). Intrinsic noise (independent per-gene transcription/translation bursts) → weak correlations (r < 0.3).  
   - **Why feasible:** The instrument reports `protein_conc` for arbitrary species and `ribosome_conc` globally; computing correlation across seeds is straightforward statistics on accessible readouts.

2. **Rival: Cell-cycle/physiological state variation**  
   - **Distinguishing test:** Normalize protein copy number by global reporters (`dry_mass`, `ribosome_conc`, `cell_mass`) and recompute CV.  
   - **Discrimination:** If variation is due to cell-size or growth-rate heterogeneity, normalization collapses CV to ≤0.1. If intrinsic noise dominates, CV remains >0.15 post-normalization.  
   - **Why feasible:** All normalizing channels (`dry_mass`, `ribosome_conc`, `cell_mass`) are available as instrument readouts; division and CV recalculation require no new measurements.

**Feasibility on this instrument:**  
- The instrument natively supports `seeds` (independent stochastic replicates) and reports protein copy number per species.  
- `wildtype/basal` is the reference design, explicitly noted.  
- 50 seeds × 10 generations × protein readout at steady state is computationally straightforward.  
- Surveying ≥10 genes spanning expression ranges (high/medium/low) is trivial given the whole-cell model tracks ~hundreds of protein species.

**Alternative 1: Use mRNA copy number instead of protein**  
- **Failure mode (less faithful):** mRNA is an *intermediate*, not the functional output. The question asks whether cells "express the same gene at the same level" — in biology, "expression level" colloquially means the phenotypic consequence (protein), not the transcript. Moreover, mRNA noise is often buffered by translational averaging, so mRNA CV may *overestimate* phenotypic variation.  
- **Failure mode (less decisive):** mRNA noise and protein noise have different timescales and amplitudes; using mRNA could confound transcriptional bursting with post-transcriptional regulation, weakening discrimination of intrinsic stochastic mechanisms.

**Alternative 2: Use mean protein level (not CV) as the test statistic**  
- **Failure mode (less faithful):** The question asks whether cells express "the same" level, which is about *variation*, not central tendency. Comparing means across seeds tests whether *populations* differ, not whether *individual cells* within a population are heterogeneous. Mean is blind to dispersion.  
- **Failure mode (less decisive):** For isogenic seeds in identical conditions, means should trivially match under both H0 and H1 (no systematic bias). The discriminating feature is the *width* of the distribution, not its center. CV directly captures this.

**Alternative 3: Test only a single high-expression constitutive gene (e.g., ribosomal protein)**  
- **Failure mode (less faithful):** The question asks about "the same gene" (singular but generic), implying the phenomenon should hold broadly. Testing one gene risks idiosyncrasy (e.g., highly expressed genes have low CV due to averaging, but rare genes are noisier). A single gene cannot represent "expression" as a general phenomenon.  
- **Failure mode (less decisive):** If only a high-copy constitutive gene is tested, H1 could be rejected spuriously (CV ≈ 0.1 due to high copy number) even though low-copy genes show CV > 0.4. Testing ≥10 genes across expression ranges prevents cherry-picking and ensures the claim is genome-representative.

**Alternative 4: Use variance instead of coefficient of variation**  
- **Failure mode (less faithful):** Variance is scale-dependent. A high-expression gene with 1000 ± 200 copies (CV = 0.2) has variance = 40,000. A low-expression gene with 10 ± 2 copies (CV = 0.2) has variance = 4. Setting a single variance threshold cannot meaningfully compare genes of different abundances. CV normalizes by mean, making it the correct scale-invariant metric for "same level" across genes.

**Alternative 5: Test at generation 1 (early transient) instead of generation 10 (steady state)**  
- **Failure mode (less faithful):** Early generations may include transient dynamics from initialization artifacts, not reflecting the cell's intrinsic stochastic steady-state behavior. The question implicitly assumes cells in a stable condition ("same environment"), which corresponds to equilibrated dynamics.  
- **Failure mode (less feasible):** Generation 1 may not have equilibrated; auxiliary assumption states "transient dynamics equilibrated by generation 10." Testing too early risks confounding initialization noise with intrinsic gene expression noise.

**Alternative 6: Use fewer seeds (e.g., 5–10) to reduce computational cost**  
- **Failure mode (less decisive):** CV estimation requires adequate sample size for statistical precision. With n=5, the standard error of CV is large (≈CV/√(2(n-1)) ≈ 0.35×CV), making it hard to distinguish CV=0.15 from CV=0.25. With n≥30, SE ≈ 0.13×CV, providing stable estimates and strong statistical power to detect differences between H0 (CV ≤ 0.05) and H1 (CV > 0.2).

## Phase 2 — Cross-examination (each reads the other, responds honestly)

### Socratic Council — **VERDICT: TIE**

- **Decisive point:** Both operationalizations test the same core phenomenon (protein-level CV across isogenic seeds at steady state), use the same statistic (coefficient of variation), the same design (wildtype/basal, 50 seeds, generation 10), and discriminate the same rivals (extrinsic noise via normalization, deterministic chaos via seed variation). The question does not specify whether the CV threshold boundary should be 0.15 (mine) or 0.2 (theirs), nor whether 20 pre-specified genes (mine) or ≥10 representative genes (theirs) better operationalizes "the same gene." These are methodological refinements, not fundamental differences in construct validity.

- **Rationale:** Our operationalizations are substantively identical in all elements that matter for construct validity: both measure protein copy number (not mRNA) across stochastic seeds (not temporal variance within a lineage), use CV (not variance) to ensure scale-invariance, normalize by protein_mass or global reporters to discriminate intrinsic from extrinsic noise, and test at steady state (generation 10). The rival-discrimination strategies are equivalent: I use protein[GENE_ID]/protein_mass normalization; they use dry_mass or ribosome_conc normalization—both isolate intrinsic noise. The differences lie in (1) CV threshold (0.15 vs 0.2) and (2) gene-panel specification (20 named genes vs ≥10 spanning expression ranges). Neither threshold is definitively "correct" (both cite single-cell literature; the true biological boundary is continuous, not discrete). Neither gene-panel approach is strictly superior: my 20-gene list is explicit and reproducible but risks omitting edge cases; their ≥10 representative approach is flexible but requires transparent selection criteria to avoid bias (a point they concede). The raw question does not specify these details, so the tie reflects that both operationalizations are equipotent translations of an under-specified question.

### Cellarium agent — **VERDICT: TIE**

- **Decisive point:** Whether the question demands genome-representative sampling (≥10 genes, flexible selection) or a fixed pre-specified gene panel (20 genes, named a priori) to adjudicate "same gene" as a generalizable biological claim versus a hypothesis about specific loci.

- **Rationale:** Both operationalizations measure the identical core phenomenon (CV of protein copy number across isogenic seeds at steady state) with nearly identical thresholds (theirs: CV ≥0.15 for ≥50% of genes; mine: CV >0.2 for >50%, median ~0.25), and both employ protein_mass normalization to discriminate intrinsic from extrinsic noise. *Impartial synthesis.* Both counsel independently returned a verdict of TIE, and their reasoning converges on the same substantive conclusion: the two operationalizations are equipotent translations of the research question rather than one being stronger than the other. Both sides agree that the operationalizations measure the identical core phenomenon—the coefficient of variation of protein copy number across isogenic seeds at steady state—using the same design, the same scale-invariant statistic, and the same protein_mass normalization to discriminate intrinsic from extrinsic noise. The only divergences either side could identify were methodological refinements rather than differences in construct validity: a CV threshold of 0.15 versus 0.2 (both within the empirical range and unlikely to reverse a verdict), and a gene-selection strategy of 20 pre-specified named genes versus ≥10 representative genes chosen flexibly. Crucially, both counsel attribute this inability to separate the operationalizations to under-specification in the raw question itself, which does not fix a threshold boundary nor clarify whether "the same gene" means a defined a priori panel (favoring reproducibility) or any representative gene (favoring flexible sampling). The question therefore could not distinguish the two operationalizations because each encodes an equally defensible interpretation of ambiguities the question leaves open.
