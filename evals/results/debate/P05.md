## Phase 1 — Opening briefs (blind; each argues against *any* other operationalization)

### Socratic Council — counsel's brief

#### Thesis

This operationalization is correct because it directly measures the **defining biological signature of phenotypic heterogeneity** — dispersion in a fitness-relevant phenotype (growth rate) across isogenic replicates under constant conditions — using the simulator's intrinsic stochasticity mechanism (random seeds) and a statistically rigorous threshold (CV ≥ 5%) that discriminates biological variation from numerical noise.

---

#### Why this operationalization is faithful to the question

**"Genetically identical cells"** → `perturbation=wildtype, same condition, varying seeds parameter`  
The wildtype perturbation ensures no genetic manipulation; the same condition (basal) ensures environmental constancy; varying seeds produces independent stochastic realizations of the same genome. This is the simulator's operationalization of isogenic replicates.

**"Behave differently"** → `coefficient of variation (CV) in growth_rate across seeds ≥ 5%`  
"Behavior" in cellular biology is phenotype; growth rate is a canonical integrative phenotype reflecting metabolism, gene expression, and resource allocation. "Differently" requires quantifying dispersion; CV (standard deviation normalized by mean) is the standard metric for phenotypic heterogeneity in single-cell studies. The 5% threshold distinguishes biologically meaningful variation from numerical solver tolerance (~2%).

**Implicit construct: "intrinsic stochasticity"** → `seed-to-seed differences in trajectory`  
The raw question asks whether *identical* cells behave differently, implying non-genetic sources of variation. The seeds parameter controls stochastic events (transcription, translation, partitioning) within the model — the mechanistic source of intrinsic noise. By holding genotype and environment constant, we isolate intrinsic stochasticity as the sole explanatory variable.

**Nothing essential is dropped:**  
The question does not specify *which* behavior, so we choose growth rate because (a) it is directly available (`growth_rate` channel), (b) it integrates over many molecular processes, making it sensitive to stochastic variation anywhere in the gene expression network, and (c) it is biologically significant (fitness proxy).

**Nothing is smuggled in:**  
We do not claim *mechanism* (which genes, which stochastic events); we claim only *phenomenon* (heterogeneity exists). The 5% threshold is justified by reference to numerical noise (~2%), not by biological priors about E. coli.

---

#### Why it is decisive

**Falsifiability:**  
H0 makes a precise null claim: CV < 5% and pairwise Welch |t| < 2 across seed subsets. This is directly testable with `disconfirm(wildtype/basal, wildtype/basal, growth_rate)` by examining `per-seed spread` and computing CV from returned statistics. The decision rule is quantitative and binary.

**Discriminating power against named rivals:**

1. **Rival: Numerical artifact** — If heterogeneity were solver noise, (a) CV would remain <2% even with many seeds, (b) `per-seed spread` would be negligible, and (c) trajectories would collapse when visualized. Our design uses 25 seeds to ensure statistical power; a CV ≥ 5% with Welch |t| ≥ 2 falsifies the artifact explanation because solver tolerance does not scale with seed count.

2. **Rival: Initial-state divergence (not ongoing stochasticity)** — If variance arose only from inherited initial conditions (e.g., dry_mass, ribosome_conc at birth), variance at generation 1 would account for variance at generation 4 (no amplification). Our design runs 4 generations and measures growth_rate at steady state (final generation). If H1 is true, we can inspect `ribosome_conc` or `protein_mass` channels to show sustained divergence across generations, falsifying the "early-only" rival. This requires the auxiliary assumption that steady state is reached by generation 4.

3. **Rival: Deterministic chaos** — Acknowledged as indistinguishable from stochastic noise within the simulation envelope. We record this as an auxiliary assumption and concede we cannot discriminate these mechanisms. However, the *phenomenon* (seed-dependent heterogeneity) is still falsifiable regardless of mechanism.

**Feasibility:**  
The design uses only `wildtype` perturbation (no gene manipulation), `basal` condition (default), `growth_rate` channel (directly available), and 25 seeds × 4 generations (computationally modest). No timeline, no knockouts, no clamping. The `disconfirm()` function directly returns Welch t and per-seed spread, so the decision rule requires no custom post-processing.

---

#### Why any other operationalization is weaker

##### Alternative 1: Measure heterogeneity in **ribosome_conc** instead of growth_rate

**Weakness: Less faithful to "behavior."**  
Ribosome concentration is a molecular-level variable, not an organismal phenotype. The raw question asks if *cells* behave differently, implying a whole-cell property. Growth rate integrates over metabolism, transcription, translation, and division — a more faithful proxy for "behavior." Ribosome_conc is one mechanism among many; focusing on it smuggles in an assumption about which molecular species matters. Additionally, ribosome_conc may show high CV due to partitioning noise at division without affecting growth rate (buffered system), leading to false positives.

##### Alternative 2: Use a **narrower threshold (CV ≥ 10%)** to define heterogeneity

**Weakness: Less falsifiable.**  
A 10% threshold makes H0 easier to accept (fail to reject), reducing statistical power. The 5% threshold is justified by the need to exceed numerical noise (~2%) by a margin sufficient to ensure biological signal. A 10% threshold would miss biologically meaningful heterogeneity in tightly regulated systems. It also lacks justification: why 10% rather than 8% or 12%? The 5% threshold is defensible as "greater than 2× numerical noise," a concrete, conservative standard.

##### Alternative 3: Use **absolute variance (SD in h⁻¹)** instead of CV

**Weakness: Less generalizable and context-dependent.**  
Standard deviation in growth_rate has units (1/h) and is scale-dependent. If we later test a different condition (e.g., acetate) where mean growth rate is lower, the same absolute SD would yield a higher CV. CV normalizes by the mean, making the threshold condition-independent. The raw question does not specify a condition, so CV is the more faithful statistic for comparing heterogeneity across contexts.

##### Alternative 4: Measure heterogeneity by **comparing different conditions (basal vs. acetate)** rather than different seeds within one condition

**Weakness: Conflates environmental and intrinsic variation.**  
Comparing basal vs. acetate changes the environment, not just the stochastic realization. Any difference in growth_rate would reflect environmental response, not intrinsic heterogeneity among genetically identical cells *in the same environment*. The raw question specifies "identical cells," implying isogenic replicates in a constant context. This alternative operationalization changes the question to "do cells respond differently to different environments?" — a different phenomenon.

##### Alternative 5: Use **visual inspection of trajectories** instead of a quantitative CV threshold

**Weakness: Not falsifiable.**  
"Visibly dispersed" is subjective and does not provide a binary decision rule. Different observers may disagree. A quantitative threshold (CV ≥ 5%, Welch |t| ≥ 2) is reproducible and allows independent verification. Visual inspection also conflates transient dynamics with steady-state heterogeneity, whereas our operationalization isolates the latter by measuring at generation 4.

##### Alternative 6: Test heterogeneity in **gene expression (mRNA or protein counts for specific genes)** instead of growth rate

**Weakness: Less decisive and more vulnerable to mechanistic confounds.**  
The `species_kinds` include `protein` and `mrna`, so we could measure CV in, say, ribosomal protein abundance. However, (a) which gene to choose? Different genes have different noise profiles; the choice is arbitrary unless justified by mechanism (which we lack). (b) Gene expression noise may not propagate to phenotype if buffered by regulatory networks, leading to false positives (high molecular noise, low phenotypic heterogeneity). Growth rate is an integrative readout less sensitive to choice of specific molecule. (c) Measuring specific species requires naming them in `params` (e.g., `{'target_genes': ['rpsA']}`), increasing design complexity and mechanistic assumption load.

---

#### Concessions

1. **CV threshold is somewhat arbitrary.**  
The 5% threshold is justified by reference to numerical noise (~2%), but the factor of 2–2.5× is a pragmatic choice, not a principled biological constant. A threshold of 3% or 7% could be equally defensible. We chose 5% as a round number exceeding noise by a comfortable margin, but this is a conventional rather than fundamental boundary.

2. **Growth rate is a proxy, not a direct measure of all "behavior."**  
The raw question does not specify *which* behavior. We chose growth rate because it is integrative and fitness-relevant, but cells could exhibit heterogeneity in other phenotypes (e.g., stress response, motility, metabolic flux) that do not strongly affect growth rate. Our operationalization claims heterogeneity *exists*, not that it exists in *all* phenotypes. A null result (CV < 5%) would falsify heterogeneity in growth rate specifically, not heterogeneity in principle.

3. **Cannot distinguish deterministic chaos from stochastic noise.**  
The seeds parameter produces seed-dependent divergence, but whether this arises from stochastic gene expression (Gillespie-style) or deterministic chaos (sensitive dependence on initial floating-point states) is not resolvable within the simulation envelope. We assume the model implements stochastic mechanisms (see `auxiliary_assumptions`), but this is an untested assumption about the simulator's internals.

4. **Generational steady state may not be reached by generation 4.**  
We assume generation 4 reflects sustained phenotype, not transient initialization effects. If the system has not equilibrated, measured CV could reflect decaying transients rather than steady-state heterogeneity. This auxiliary assumption is necessary but not directly validated in the design; inspecting earlier generations (1, 2, 3) could test convergence but is not part of the core falsification.

5. **Single-channel focus may miss multidimensional heterogeneity.**  
We measure only `growth_rate`, but cells could be heterogeneous in correlated ways (e.g., high growth rate + low ppGpp, low growth rate + high ppGpp). A multivariate operationalization (e.g., PCA on growth_rate, ribosome_conc, ppgpp_conc) might capture richer structure, but it sacrifices simplicity and introduces interpretation challenges (which principal component represents "behavior"?). ### Cellarium agent — counsel's brief

#### Thesis

This operationalization is correct because it isolates intrinsic biological stochasticity from genetic uniformity by measuring between-replicate variance in quantitative phenotypes across independent stochastic seeds, using coefficient of variation as a noise-normalized metric that directly tests whether identically initialized cells diverge beyond measurement error.

#### Why this operationalization is faithful to the question

**"Genetically identical"** maps to `wildtype/basal` with constant `params={}` across all seeds—no gene knockouts, no heterologous genes, no TF clamps. The genome is invariant.

**"Cells"** maps to individual simulation trajectories, each initialized from an independent `seed` (n≥20), representing isogenic lineages that share no random-number state.

**"Behave"** maps to three core phenotypic observables:
- `growth_rate` (1/h): the integrated readout of metabolic flux, biosynthesis, and cell-cycle progression
- `dry_mass` (fg): total biomass accumulation, reflecting growth physiology
- `ribosome_conc` (μM): molecular-level proxy for translational capacity, sensitive to stochastic partitioning and gene expression noise

**"Differently"** maps to **between-seed variance** quantified as coefficient of variation (CV = std/mean), measured at generation 10 after sufficient time for intrinsic noise to amplify. The threshold CV > 0.05 operationalizes "different" as variance exceeding technical noise (which would produce CV ≤ 0.05 in a deterministic system with only floating-point error).

Nothing is dropped: the question asks whether genetic identity constrains behavior, and the operationalization tests whether identical genomes in identical environments produce identical trajectories. Nothing is smuggled in: we measure only the phenotypes the instrument provides, without assuming mechanisms (e.g., we don't require knowing *which* stochastic processes—transcription bursts, partitioning noise—drive divergence).

#### Why it is decisive

**Falsifiability:** H1 predicts CV ≥ 0.05 in at least one channel at generation 10; H0 predicts CV ≤ 0.05 in all. The decision rule is binary and pre-specified: if CV(growth_rate) < 0.05 AND ANOVA p > 0.05, reject H1. This is a crisp, computable test.

**Discriminating power against rivals:**

1. **Technical noise artifact:** Rival predicts CV is generation-invariant (constant across gen 5, 10, 15) and condition-invariant (basal vs acetate). Our design includes temporal controls (3 generation timepoints) and environmental controls (acetate condition). If CV increases with generation and differs by condition, we confirm biological amplification, not measurement artifact.

2. **Deterministic chaos:** Rival predicts exponential CV growth (positive Lyapunov exponent) and high initial-condition sensitivity. Our design can detect exponential vs linear CV(generation) scaling by fitting log(CV) vs generation. If CV grows sublinearly or saturates, chaos is refuted in favor of bounded stochastic noise.

**Feasibility:** All channels (growth_rate, dry_mass, ribosome_conc) are listed in `instrument_dial_labels`. The `wildtype/basal` design is the explicit `reference_design`. Seeds and generations are native schema parameters. The CV statistic requires only mean/std computation across seeds at a fixed generation. ANOVA is standard. No custom perturbations, no unavailable measurements.

#### Why any other operationalization is weaker

**Alternative 1: Single-seed time-series variance (CV across time within one replicate)**
- **Failure mode:** Conflates temporal autocorrelation (transient fluctuations around a deterministic attractor) with true divergence of independent trajectories. A deterministic cell with periodic oscillations would show high temporal CV but zero between-replicate CV. This operationalization cannot distinguish "different cells" (the question's plural) from "one cell fluctuating."

**Alternative 2: Snapshot variance at generation 1 (early divergence)**
- **Failure mode:** Insufficient amplification time. If intrinsic noise sources (e.g., partitioning at division, low-copy-number molecules) require multiple generations to accumulate detectable variance, generation 1 measurements would fall below the 0.05 threshold even for truly stochastic cells, yielding false negatives. Generation 10 balances amplification time against simulator feasibility.

**Alternative 3: Broader phenotype (e.g., RNA mass, protein mass, cell mass)**
- **Failure mode:** RNA mass and protein mass are compositional readouts, not integrative measures of cellular "behavior." Growth rate integrates metabolism, biosynthesis, and division timing—the functional output of cellular decisions. Mass channels are less sensitive discriminators because they may be tightly regulated (homeostatic) even when growth rate varies. Ribosome concentration, being mechanistically upstream of growth, offers complementary molecular resolution. Using only bulk mass channels would miss molecular stochasticity.

**Alternative 4: Narrower phenotype (single channel, e.g., ribosome_conc only)**
- **Failure mode:** The question asks if "cells behave differently," not "ribosomes vary." Behavior is multi-dimensional. A single channel risks false negatives if that channel happens to be clamped by regulatory feedback while others diverge. Three channels (growth_rate, dry_mass, ribosome_conc) spanning scales (population, bulk biomass, molecular) ensure robust detection of heterogeneity wherever it manifests.

**Alternative 5: Continuous distribution test (e.g., Kolmogorov-Smirnov) instead of CV threshold**
- **Failure mode:** KS tests detect *any* distributional difference, including bimodality, skew, or outliers—phenomena not implied by "behave differently" in the sense of variance. The question's natural reading is about *dispersion* (do cells spread out?), not shape. CV + ANOVA directly test dispersion. KS would be oversensitive to irrelevant distribution features and undersensitive to pure variance shifts.

**Alternative 6: Pairwise trajectory distance (e.g., Euclidean distance in phenotype space)**
- **Failure mode:** More computationally expensive, less interpretable, and obscures the signal. CV is dimensionless and noise-normalized; raw distances depend on arbitrary scales (1/h vs fg vs μM) and require multivariate normalization. For falsification, a single scalar (CV) per channel is clearer than a distance matrix. The gain in "holistic" trajectory comparison does not justify the loss of interpretability.

**Alternative 7: Condition perturbation as primary design (e.g., basal vs acetate as main contrast)**
- **Failure mode:** This tests "do cells behave differently *in different environments*?" not "do genetically identical cells behave differently *despite identical genetics and environment*?" The question's force is about intrinsic stochasticity under clamped conditions. Using acetate as the reference muddies the interpretation: variance could be attributed to environmental sensitivity rather than intrinsic noise. Our operationalization uses acetate as a *discriminating control* (to reject the technical-noise rival), not as the main target.

#### Concessions

**Debatable choice: CV threshold of 0.05 as the noise floor.** This value is heuristic. If the simulator's numerical precision is higher (or lower) than assumed, the threshold may be too lenient (or stringent). We inherit this from standard experimental practice (CV ~ 5% is typical replicate noise), but without empirical calibration runs on this specific simulator, the boundary between "biological" and "technical" variance is stipulated, not validated.

**Vulnerability: Deterministic chaos vs stochastic noise.** Our auxiliary assumptions concede we cannot fully resolve whether observed variance is intrinsic stochasticity or deterministic chaos *without access to the simulator's RNG internals*. The temporal scaling analysis (CV vs generation) helps, but if chaos dominates, our "stochastic heterogeneity" interpretation is mechanistically wrong, even if the empirical claim (cells diverge) holds. We operationalize "intrinsic noise" as *any variance arising from internal dynamics*, agnostic to its deterministic vs stochastic substrate.

**Generation 10 endpoint is arbitrary.** Longer runs (generation 20, 50) might reveal saturation or continued divergence, but we trade off amplification time against simulator cost and the risk of confounding long-term drift. If divergence saturates by generation 10, our operationalization correctly detects it; if divergence is sub-threshold at gen 10 but emerges later, we risk false negatives. The inclusion of gen 5 and gen 15 controls mitigates this, but the focal timepoint remains a pragmatic guess.

**Reliance on wildtype/basal as "constant environment."** If the simulator's basal condition harbors hidden temporal drift (e.g., slow metabolite accumulation, implicit circadian-like oscillations), our "constant environment" assumption fails, and observed variance could reflect uncontrolled environmental heterogeneity. We assume the simulator implements basal as truly static, but cannot verify this from the schema alone.

**Sample size n=30 may be underpowered for small effects.** If true biological CV is near 0.06 (just above threshold), 30 seeds may lack statistical power to reliably reject H0. A power analysis would require knowing the simulator's true noise magnitude, which we don't have. The choice of n=30 is a reasonable compromise between cost and precision, but not optimized.

### Cellarium agent — **VERDICT: TIE**

- **Decisive point:** The question does not specify whether "behave differently" requires demonstrating amplification of heterogeneity over generational time (my temporal divergence signature) or simply detecting steady-state dispersion at a defined endpoint (their equilibrium variance). Both operationalizations measure between-seed CV in growth_rate at wildtype/basal with n≥20, CV threshold ≥0.05, and ANOVA/Welch-t; the sole substantive difference is generation 10 vs generation 4 and whether temporal scaling (CV vs generation slope) is required for the claim.

- **Rationale:** Our operationalizations are methodologically equivalent at their core: both measure coefficient of variation in growth_rate across independent wildtype/basal seeds, use CV ≥ 0.05 as the biological-vs-noise threshold, and employ parametric tests (ANOVA vs Welch-t, which are statistically cognate for this design). ## Outcome

**Cellarium agent prevails.** The Socratic Council conceded that the Cellarium agent's operationalization is stronger; the agent did not concede.

*Impartial synthesis.* The two verdicts diverge on whether the question can separate the operationalizations. The Socratic Council yields, judging the opposing multi-channel design (growth_rate, dry_mass, ribosome_conc) with temporal controls at generations 5, 10, and 15 to be decisively stronger, because measuring whether variance grows across generations distinguishes biological stochasticity from technical noise, and because "behave differently" is inherently multidimensional—advantages its own single-channel, generation-4 design lacks. The Cellarium agent, by contrast, sees the core methods as equivalent (between-seed CV in growth_rate, threshold ≥0.05, cognate parametric tests) and treats the seed counts and endpoints as quantitative dial-turns, locating the only substantive difference in an unresolved ambiguity: whether "behave differently" demands dynamically amplified heterogeneity over generations or merely static dispersion at equilibrium. Because the agent argues each operationalization is stronger only under a different reading of the question that the raw prompt does not fix, its TIE rests on that interpretive underdetermination rather than on rebutting the multidimensional-coverage point that drove the Council's concession. Since the two sides do not converge—one conceding on discriminating power and channel breadth, the other holding that the question fails to adjudicate between temporal and equilibrium framings—the debate does not yield an agreed winner, and the outcome turns on whether one accepts that the question specifies dynamic amplification as part of "behaving differently."
