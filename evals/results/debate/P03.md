## Phase 1 — Opening briefs (blind; each argues against *any* other operationalization)

### Socratic Council — counsel's brief

#### Thesis
This operationalization is correct because it maps the construct of antibiotic-tolerant persisters onto **population-level heterogeneity in growth_rate**, directly tests the mechanistic hypothesis of ppGpp-mediated entry via **conditional statistics on low-growth subpopulations**, and discriminates stochastic phenotype switching from deterministic cell-cycle effects and genetic drift through **timeline perturbation response** and **cross-generational stability**.

---

#### Why this operationalization is faithful to the question

The raw question asks why bacteria survive antibiotics **without becoming resistant** — the critical constructs are:
1. **Survival without genetic change** → operationalized as phenotypic heterogeneity within an **isogenic population** (wildtype, same seeds measure stochastic variation only, no mutation).
2. **Some bacteria** → operationalized as a **subpopulation fraction** with growth_rate < 0.1× mean, not all-or-none.
3. **Survive antibiotics** → proxied by **low growth_rate**, the established metabolic correlate of antibiotic tolerance (antibiotics target active growth processes; the simulator lacks explicit antibiotic survival, acknowledged in auxiliary assumptions as scope limitation but mechanistically justified).
4. **The mechanism** → operationalized via **ppGpp_conc** (stringent response alarmone) and **ribosome_conc** (translation machinery downregulation), the molecular hallmarks of the persister state in literature.

The operational definitions are exhaustive:
- **Persister state** = growth_rate < 0.1× mean (quantitative threshold for "low growth").
- **Stringent response activation** = ppGpp_conc ≥2× mean in low-growth cells (causal driver).
- **Ribosome downregulation** = ribosome_conc ≤0.5× mean in low-growth cells (downstream consequence).
- **Starvation trigger** = amino-acid removal via timeline (environmental condition known to induce persistence).

Nothing essential is dropped: the question's "why" is addressed by the ppGpp/ribosome mechanism and starvation modulation. Nothing is smuggled: genetic resistance is explicitly excluded (isogenic population), and antibiotic exposure is transparently proxied by growth_rate with justification.

---

#### Why it is decisive

**Falsifiability**: H0 states all cells are statistically uniform (unimodal, tight distribution). H1 predicts a **bimodal distribution** with ≥5% in the low-growth tail, detectable by Hartigan dip test (p<0.05). The dual criterion (fraction ≥5% AND bimodality) prevents false positives from mere tail noise. The ppGpp fold-change (≥2×, Welch t≥2) provides a second independent falsifier for mechanism, not just correlation.

**Discriminating power**:
- **Rival #1 (cell-cycle artifact)**: The timeline design (amino-acid removal at generation 5) is the discriminating experiment. If low growth is merely S-phase, the fraction stays constant (~15%, typical S-phase fraction); if persisters are starvation-induced, the fraction jumps to 25–40%. This is a **qualitative difference** testable on this instrument (timeline perturbation is explicitly supported: "media-shift events over time… aa shift").
  
- **Rival #2 (ppGpp correlation, not causation)**: The ppgpp_conc/multiplier=1.0 design clamps ppGpp to basal, blocking starvation-induced elevation. If ppGpp is causal, this clamp abolishes the starvation-induced persister increase (fraction <10% vs. 25–40% unclamped). If ppGpp is passive, clamping does nothing. This is a **causal intervention** uniquely enabled by this instrument's ppgpp_conc perturbation.

- **Rival #3 (epigenetic/mutant accumulation)**: Comparing fraction at generation 3 vs. generation 8 (same design, vary generations parameter) tests temporal accumulation. Measuring inter-seed CV tests heritable lineage effects. H1 predicts stable fraction and CV<50%; rival #3 predicts increasing fraction and high CV. This is **feasible** (vary generations, measure per-seed spread returned by falsification call).

**Feasibility**: All channels (growth_rate, ppGpp_conc, ribosome_conc) are explicit dial labels. #### Why any other operationalization is weaker

**Alternative 1: Measure only population-mean ppGpp, ignore growth heterogeneity.**  
This conflates the persister subpopulation with the bulk. The question asks why **some** bacteria survive — population means erase the critical minority. A weak ppGpp increase in the mean (e.g., 1.2× under starvation) could reflect a small high-ppGpp subpopulation diluted by the majority, or a uniform modest increase in all cells. **Failure mode**: cannot distinguish subpopulation from population-wide shift; cannot test the "some bacteria" construct. This operationalization is **less faithful** (loses heterogeneity) and **less falsifiable** (rival #1, cell-cycle artifact, is indistinguishable from a uniform response).

**Alternative 2: Use ribosome_conc alone as the persister marker, omit ppGpp.**  
Ribosome downregulation is a **consequence** of the stringent response, not the trigger. Without ppGpp, we cannot test the mechanistic claim or distinguish rival #2 (ppGpp as passive marker vs. causal driver). **Failure mode**: descriptive only, no causal handle. Ribosome concentration could drop for many reasons (nutrient limitation reducing translation globally); ppGpp elevation is the **specific molecular signature** of the stringent response that links starvation to persistence. This is **less decisive** (cannot refute rival #2) and **less faithful** (misses the proposed mechanism).

**Alternative 3: Define persisters as growth_rate < 0.01× mean (stricter threshold).**  
A 100-fold reduction may be too extreme for the simulator's noise floor or biological realism; the predicted 5–15% fraction would collapse to <1%, below detectable signal (per-seed variation might dominate). **Failure mode**: underpowered, high false-negative rate. Conversely, growth_rate < 0.5× mean (looser threshold) would capture too many cells (likely >30% even in basal, given stochastic noise), blurring the subpopulation and making the starvation effect undetectable. **Failure mode**: conflates stochastic stragglers with true persisters, loses specificity. The 0.1× threshold balances sensitivity (5–15% detectable) and specificity (clearly below noise). Alternative thresholds are **less feasible** (too noisy or too rare).

**Alternative 4: Operationalize "survival" as dry_mass or protein_mass instead of growth_rate.**  
Persisters are defined by **growth arrest**, not by mass (a non-growing cell retains mass). Measuring dry_mass would fail to detect metabolically dormant cells unless they shrink, which is not the canonical phenotype. **Failure mode**: conflates growth arrest with starvation-induced mass loss; mass is a stock, growth_rate is the flow that antibiotics target. This is **less faithful** (growth arrest is the construct, not mass) and **less decisive** (mass may not correlate with antibiotic survival; growth_rate is the direct mechanistic link).

**Alternative 5: Use gene_knockout of relA as the primary test, not timeline/starvation.**  
A relA knockout (ppGpp synthesis ablation) tests whether ppGpp is **necessary**, but does not test stochastic entry or starvation induction. Under basal conditions, even wildtype may show low basal ppGpp, so relA KO vs. wildtype may show no difference in persister fraction (both near zero persisters without stress). **Failure mode**: tests necessity in the wrong regime (basal, not starvation); cannot test starvation modulation (rival #1 not addressed). This is **less decisive** (no discriminating power against cell-cycle artifact) and **less faithful** (ignores the "starvation trigger" part of the mechanism).

**Alternative 6: Measure fraction_trna_charged instead of ppGpp.**  
Uncharged tRNA triggers ppGpp synthesis (via RelA), so it is **upstream** of ppGpp. However, tRNA charging reflects amino-acid availability, not the stringent response output. Multiple pathways affect tRNA charging; ppGpp is the **specific** alarmone. **Failure mode**: less specific marker, cannot distinguish stringent response from other translation stresses. This is **less faithful** (ppGpp is the named mechanism in the claim) and **less decisive** (tRNA charging does not discriminate rival #2, since clamping ppGpp affects downstream processes, not tRNA directly).
