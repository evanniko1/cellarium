## Phase 1 — Opening briefs (blind; each argues against *any* other operationalization)

### Socratic Council — counsel's brief

#### Thesis
This operationalization is correct because it measures the complete causal chain (tRNA charging → ppGpp → ribosome synthesis → growth) across a natural gradient of nutrient quality, enabling both correlation-based screening and perturbation-based causal tests that isolate ppGpp's mechanistic role.

#### Why this operationalization is faithful to the question

The raw question asks "how does a cell **decide** how many ribosomes to make in a **given medium**"—three constructs require operationalization:

1. **"Decide how many ribosomes"** → `ribosome_conc` (active ribosome concentration, uM). This directly quantifies the regulatory outcome. The channel measures active ribosomes, not just rRNA transcripts or structural components, capturing the functional decision endpoint.

2. **"In a given medium"** → five static carbon-source conditions (`basal`, `acetate`, `fumarate`, `malate`, `succinate`). These span a natural metabolic gradient without confounding temporal dynamics (no timeline shifts). The conditions are simulator primitives, ensuring reproducibility.

3. **"How does a cell decide"** (mechanism) → the mediating pathway: `fraction_trna_charged` (nutrient sensor) → `ppgpp_conc` (signaling intermediate) → `ribosome_conc` (output) → `growth_rate` (phenotypic consequence). Each node maps to a distinct dial label, making the causal chain empirically traceable.

Nothing essential is dropped: the question implies a **regulatory decision** (not passive drift), which H1 specifies as active feedback; a **response to medium** (not genetic reprogramming), operationalized as static condition variation; and a **mechanistic explanation**, formalized as the ppGpp-mediated stringent response pathway. No extraneous constructs are smuggled in—growth_rate is included only as a downstream validator, not as the decision itself.

#### Why it is decisive

**Falsifiability**: H0 states ribosome concentration is independent of ppGpp and tRNA charging. The decision rule has two independent criteria:
- **Effect test**: Welch t ≥ 2.0 AND Cohen d ≥ 0.8 for acetate vs. basal ribosome_conc (n=8 each). This rules out noise-driven differences.
- **Regression test**: OLS slope 95% CI excludes zero AND R² ≥ 0.80 across ≥4 conditions. This rules out spurious or weak correlations.

Both must pass to confirm H1. If either fails, H1 is refuted. The two-pronged test prevents Type I errors from multiple comparisons while ensuring biological meaningfulness (effect size threshold).

**Discriminating power against rivals**:
- **Rival 1 (constitutive program, no ppGpp causality)**: The `ppgpp_conc` clamp (2× basal ppGpp in basal medium) decouples ppGpp from tRNA charging. If H1 is true, clamped ppGpp should reduce ribosome_conc even when fraction_trna_charged remains high. If Rival 1 is true, ribosome_conc is unchanged, proving ppGpp is non-causal. This is a clean manipulative test unavailable to observation alone.
  
- **Rival 2 (passive dilution, no active repression)**: The `rrna_operon_knockout` in basal medium tests whether ribosome_conc reduction is active (via ppGpp elevation) or passive (via growth dilution). If H1 is true, the knockout elevates ppGpp and drops ribosome_conc disproportionately to growth_rate. If Rival 2 is true, they scale proportionally with no ppGpp change. The perturbation isolates the control logic.

**Feasibility**: All channels (`ribosome_conc`, `fraction_trna_charged`, `ppgpp_conc`, `growth_rate`) are instrument primitives. All perturbations (`wildtype`, `ppgpp_conc`, `rrna_operon_knockout`) are listed in the instrument spec. The five carbon sources are static-only options. The design uses only 7 perturbation-condition pairs, each with 8 seeds × 8 generations = modest computational cost. Steady-state sampling (final 20% of generations) is operationally clear.

#### Why any other operationalization is weaker

**Alternative 1: Measure rRNA transcription rate instead of ribosome_conc**  
*Failure mode*: The instrument offers no `rrna_transcript_rate` channel. Even if inferred from `mrna` species (rRNA genes), this measures synthesis but not the functional decision—post-transcriptional processing, ribosome assembly, and degradation decouple synthesis from active ribosome abundance. The question asks how many ribosomes are *made* (functional units), not how much rRNA is transcribed. Measuring synthesis alone would miss regulatory steps downstream of transcription.

**Alternative 2: Use a broader nutrient gradient (amino acid shifts via timeline perturbations)**  
*Failure mode*: The question specifies "in a **given medium**" (static condition), not dynamic responses to shifts. Timeline perturbations (e.g., `amino_acid_shift`) introduce temporal confounders (adaptation kinetics, memory effects, transient spikes) that obscure the steady-state regulatory setpoint. Static conditions isolate the decision logic from stress-response dynamics. Moreover, timeline shifts cannot be combined with static carbon sources (per instrument note), limiting the metabolic range. The five static carbon sources are the maximal feasible gradient that respects "given medium."

**Alternative 3: Measure only the correlation (regression test) without perturbations**  
*Failure mode*: Correlation cannot distinguish H1 from Rival 1 (epiphenomenal correlation) or Rival 2 (dilution). Without the ppGpp clamp, any correlation between ribosome_conc and fraction_trna_charged is consistent with a third variable (e.g., ATP availability) driving both. Without the rrna_operon_knockout, the direction of causality is ambiguous. Observation-only designs sacrifice discriminatory power; the current operationalization's strength is the **dual-mode test** (correlation + manipulation).

**Alternative 4: Use a narrower condition set (only basal vs. acetate)**  
*Failure mode*: Two points cannot establish a quantitative relationship (the predicted slope 1.5–2.0 is untestable). A dichotomous comparison risks idiosyncrasy—acetate may have off-target effects (e.g., pH stress) unrelated to tRNA charging. The five-condition gradient (basal, acetate, fumarate, malate, succinate) provides statistical power for regression, ensures the relationship is graded (not binary), and cross-validates that the tRNA-charging axis, not acetate-specific toxicity, drives the effect.

**Alternative 5: Use `dry_mass` or `protein_mass` instead of `ribosome_conc`**  
*Failure mode*: These are whole-cell aggregates, diluting the specific regulatory signal. Ribosome protein is ~20–30% of total protein; conflating ribosome abundance with bulk protein masks the decision. The question asks about **ribosome** production, not general biosynthesis. Using `ribosome_conc` directly tests the construct without noise from unrelated protein pools.

**Alternative 6: Define "decide" as a binary threshold (ribosome_conc > X) instead of quantitative scaling**  
*Failure mode*: The hypothesis predicts a graded, ~linear relationship (slope 1.5–2.0), not a switch. A binary operationalization would test a qualitatively different claim (bistability) and would be less falsifiable (arbitrary threshold choice). The quantitative regression is more faithful to "how does a cell decide"—implying a dose-response, not an all-or-none decision.

#### Concessions

1. **Linear assumption vulnerability**: The predicted slope (1.5–2.0) and linearity (R² ≥ 0.80) are strong commitments. If the true relationship is saturating (Michaelis-Menten-like) or threshold-gated, the regression may fail even if ppGpp causally mediates the response. A nonlinear model (e.g., hyperbolic fit) might be more faithful, but the operationalization chooses linear regression for falsifiability—non-linearity would require additional shape parameters, reducing decisiveness.

2. **Steady-state assumption**: Sampling only the final 20% of generations assumes equilibrium is reached by generation 8. If some conditions exhibit slow oscillations or bistability, the "steady-state" reading may be phase-dependent. The operationalization does not include a convergence diagnostic (e.g., coefficient of variation across the final window). This is a debatable simplification for computational tractability.

3. **ppGpp clamp mechanism unknown**: The operationalization assumes `ppgpp_conc` perturbation (2× basal) is implemented as a direct concentration clamp, not via upstream RelA/SpoT manipulation. If the simulator instead modulates ppGpp synthesis/degradation rates, the decoupling test (Rival 1) may be confounded by homeostatic feedback. The instrument spec does not clarify the implementation, so this perturbation's validity is assumed, not verified.

4. **rrna_operon_knockout scope**: Deleting one rRNA operon (out of seven in *E. coli*) may produce only a modest ribosome reduction, weakening the signal for Rival 2's test. A stronger test would delete more operons, but the operationalization specifies `operons_to_delete: 1` for conservatism (avoiding lethality). If the effect is too weak, the disproportionate ppGpp elevation may be undetectable, failing to distinguish active repression from dilution.

5. **Condition gradient completeness**: The five static carbon sources (acetate, fumarate, malate, succinate) are the only alternatives to basal listed as `static_only_carbon`. If this set does not span a wide enough range in `fraction_trna_charged` (e.g., all cluster near basal), the regression will lack dynamic range. The operationalization assumes these conditions are sufficiently diverse, but this is an empirical bet, not guaranteed by the instrument spec.

6. **Confounding by growth rate**: If ribosome_conc correlates with fraction_trna_charged solely because both correlate with growth_rate (Rival 2's claim), the regression test alone does not adjudicate. The operationalization relies on the rrna_operon_knockout to break the confound, but if that perturbation is not mechanistically simulated (as the note warns), the rival remains untested. The ppGpp clamp provides partial backup, but full causal closure depends on perturbation fidelity.

## Phase 2 — Cross-examination (each reads the other, responds honestly)

### Socratic Council — **VERDICT: TIE**

- **Decisive point:** The question does not specify whether "decide" requires demonstrating *necessity* of the ppGpp pathway (via knockout) or merely establishing the *quantitative input-output relationship* (via gradient). Both operationalizations measure the same core chain (tRNA charging → ppGpp → ribosome_conc → growth_rate) and test it across the same media conditions; they differ only in whether the relA/spoT knockout is mandatory to answer "how does a cell decide."

- **Rationale:** Both operationalizations measure ribosome_conc across four shared static carbon sources (basal, acetate, fumarate, succinate) with identical observables (ribosome_conc, fraction_trna_charged, ppgpp_conc, growth_rate), identical n=8 seeds, and near-identical statistical tests (OLS regression requiring R²≥0.80–0.85, t-tests with effect-size thresholds). My operationalization predicts ribosome_conc scales with fraction_trna_charged (slope 1.5–2.0); theirs predict ribosome_conc scales with growth_rate (slope ~40 µM per 1/h). My operationalization answers "how" by tracing the quantitative dose-response (input: nutrient quality via tRNA charging; output: ribosome_conc) and uses perturbations (ppGpp clamp, rRNA knockout) to rule out rival *mechanisms* (passive dilution, constitutive transcription).
