Cellarium · a glass box over whole-cell reasoning

Turning an opaque cell simulator into trustworthy science.

A whole-cell model of E. coli is mechanistic, genome-complete — and effectively a black box. Cellarium reads it through a discipline: a blind Socratic Council that frames falsifiable hypotheses without seeing the data, and a grounded Cellwright agent that tests them against real simulations and the literature — under a provenance guard that separates what the model was fitted to from what it genuinely predicts.

Two ways in: Mode A — the Council invents a falsifiable hypothesis blind, then hands it over; Mode B — you ask Cellwright a data question directly. Both answer only through grounded tools.

00the object

What the model actually is

Before reading it, know its size. The wcEcoli model (Macklin et al., Science 2020) is a single E. coli cell written as a coupled dynamical system — transcription, translation, replication, metabolism and regulation advanced together on a one-second clock, one molecule count at a time.

Every run emits a full molecular trace — species counts, fluxes and rates at each of ~10⁴ steps — so a single simulation is 0.6 to 2.5 GB on disk (§07). Growth is not an input: the homeostatic metabolism keeps ~173 pools in range, and division emerges.

01the instrument

A blind council and a grounded wright

Two named halves, each chosen for what it guards against.

The Socratic Council takes its name from the Socratic method: a Proposer advances a claim, a Skeptic attacks it, and a Judge distils a single falsifiable hypothesis with a pre-registered falsifier — all blind to the simulation data. Framing the test before seeing the numbers is what prevents hypothesising after the results are known.

Cellwright is a wright — a maker, as in ship-wright or play-wright — one who works the cell. It is the grounded craftsman: it asserts nothing from memory, only through 38 tools over the corpus, the raw simulation traces and the literature — statistics, differential expression, viability, provenance, regulon and flux reads, literature retrieval. A provenance guard tags every design in-sample (fitted) versus out-of-sample (predicted), and reference control picks the matched comparison, so a claim can be audited rather than taken on faith.

Every investigation persists to a local SQLite store: the agent's full message history and tool calls survive a restart, and a fresh clone of the project boots pre-loaded with the recorded Council runs and investigations.

02the method in two arcs

Where the discipline changes the answer

One loop per mode.

Mode A · Council-framed, blind — a falsification
H
Council · blind, pre-registered

An aminoacyl-tRNA-synthetase knockout should raise ppGpp 2–4×.

Textbook stringent response: uncharged tRNA activates RelA, ppGpp climbs. The Judge pre-registers the falsifier — "refute if ppGpp < 0.8× wild type" — before any data is seen.

T
Cellwright · grounded test

The corpus meets the falsifier decisively.

Across four aaRS knockouts, ppGpp collapses ~90% and RelA ~97% — the opposite direction. argS: 6.5 vs 64 µM, −90%, t=−27.8.

M
Mechanism · deep-dive

RelA is expression-coupled, so it dilutes when translation stalls.

Charged-tRNA barely moves (−5%): RelA is never activated by an uncharged-tRNA signal — it simply cannot be made once translation halts. The downstream arm is intact (a ppGpp clamp still represses ribosomes, −15%); only the upstream sensing is miswired.

Judge · verdict

Blind pre-registration turns "the ppGpp looks low" into a decisive falsification of the model's stringent sensing — systematic (4 of 4 knockouts, |t| up to 47), mechanistically explained, and bounded. A sighted agent would only have described the low ppGpp. [Winther, Roghanian & Gerdes 2018; Traxler 2008]

Mode B · direct data question — a control flips the finding
Q
User → Cellwright · direct

Does nitrate switch on the nitrate-reductase genes?

Asked straight to Cellwright, no Council. First pass against aerobic growth: narG +2.15 (q=0.004), narH +1.66 — apparently a clean yes.

S
Cellwright's own skeptic step

But the nitrate condition also removes oxygen.

Anaerobiosis alone drives many of the same genes. The honest control is an anaerobic reference without nitrate — re-run against no_oxygen.

T
Cellwright · reference-controlled

Controlled, narGHJI no longer moves — the real signal is repression.

The nitrate-specific effect is repression of fermentation (fumarate reductase, mixed-acid genes): the NarL respiratory hierarchy.

05a clash that led somewhere

The numbers, the clash, and the pharmacology it opened

The glass box working end to end: a model result collides with textbook theory, the collision frames a wet-lab experiment, and a grounded literature search reveals both a known biology and a gap no one has filled.

the result · cut the NUMBERS

Delete operons → fewer ribosomes, and the cell copes

  • Graded, not catastrophic: ribosome −50%, growth −35% at six of seven operons removed; still dividing for four generations.
  • The remaining operons compensate — per-operon rRNA output rises 1.6× → 3.3× — exactly the ribosome-feedback up-regulation Condon measured. [Condon 1993]
  • And with ppGpp flat (~62 µM), matching Condon's finding that this compensation is ppGpp-independent. The model gets the mechanism right.
The clash — numbers versus efficiency
what we did · numbers

Fewer ribosome genes

Deleting operons removes the genes, so the cell physically cannot build more ribosomes — its supply is capped.

ribosomes ↓ as growth ↓
Scott's 2nd law · efficiency

Slower ribosomes (chloramphenicol)

Jamming each ribosome leaves the genes intact, so the cell over-builds to compensate — ribosome fraction rises as growth falls.

ribosomes ↑ as growth ↓

Same endpoint — fewer functional ribosomes, slower growth — reached two ways, yet the cell's response is opposite, because only the efficiency route leaves it free to compensate. Our numbers-axis result sits on the far side of Scott's second law: not a contradiction, a different axis.

H
the hypothesis the clash generates

Do both at once — cap the numbers and jam the efficiency.

A cell that cannot build more ribosomes, hit with a drug that slows the ones it has, should have no way to compensate — predicting a synergistic, non-linear collapse. A clean experiment falls out: an operon-deletion series crossed with a translation inhibitor.

L
Cellwright · targeted literature search

The experiment has a name — the "Numbers Game."

Levin et al. built E. coli with 1–6 of the 7 rrn operons deleted and challenged them with chloramphenicol, tetracycline and azithromycin: operon-poor strains are killed far faster — but not by ciprofloxacin, which does not target ribosomes. The synergy is real and experimentally confirmed. [Levin 2017, mBio]

the contribution

The Numbers Game is established biology — but it has never been shown computationally in a whole-cell model. Cellarium already reproduces the numbers half (the dose-response and Condon's compensation); the drug-synergy prediction is a clean, falsifiable validation target — and its natural scale, a growing colony under a drug gradient, is exactly what Vivarium (many wcEcoli cells in a shared environment, already used for tetracycline) can run. It also opens a pharmacology angle: predicting a ribosome-targeting antibiotic's potency from a cell's ribosome-allocation state. The clash did not just recover biology we had forgotten — it pointed at a model test worth running. [Vivarium: Agmon et al. 2022, Bioinformatics · wcEcoli colonies: Skalnik et al. 2023, PLoS Comput Biol]

07the scale problem

Why the deep dives need the full corpus

A whole-cell simulation is not a row in a table; it is a molecular movie. That gap is the reason for the two-tier data design.

38
Cellwright tools

statistics, differential, viability, regulon and flux reads, provenance, literature retrieval.

SQLite
conversation persistence

full agent history per investigation; a clone boots pre-loaded.

239
runs · one shard

distilled to a 5 MB DuckDB / Parquet manifest that ships in-repo.

Data per question — distilled vs raw
DuckDB shard (all 239)5 MB
one raw run (simOut)0.6–2.5 GB
full raw corpus (HF)198 GB

The distilled shard — summary channels, viability, pathways, panel species — answers most questions with no download and no model image. A deep dive (an arbitrary protein's trajectory, a regulon response, an exchange flux) needs the full per-timestep trace, which lives only in the raw simOut. Because each run reaches gigabytes, the raw corpus is published as a 197.9 GB Hugging Face dataset (96 archives) and pulled on demand — the shard for breadth, the corpus for depth. That is what makes the deep dives in §02 and §05 possible.

08the horizon

The map is also a to-do list

Cellarium does not just grade a whole-cell model — it hands its builders a ranked, mechanistically-attributed list of what to fix next, and lets everyone else question the model in plain language and trust the answer.

Whole-cell modelers

A provenance-controlled audit that turns "is it good?" into specific, attributed fixes: the missing inducible regulation, A-site RelA sensing, the FBA objective.

Systems & synthetic biologists

Interrogate a mechanistic cell without the compute stack — and learn what to trust it for: global physiology yes, inducible circuits and lethality not yet.

Experimental microbiologists

Prioritised, falsifiable, quantitative bench hypotheses — the rRNA dose-response of §05 being the exemplar.

The AI-for-science method

Blind hypothesis generation plus grounded testing plus provenance is a general recipe for trustworthy claims from any rich simulator.

Close the boundaries

Treat the §04 boundaries as a research agenda, with Cellarium as the regression harness that verifies each fix.

Grow & open the corpus

"The Well for the cell": more designs, more depth on Hugging Face, as a community benchmark and a substrate for ML surrogates.

The Numbers Game, computationally

Reproduce Levin's rRNA-operon × translation-inhibitor synergy in a wcEcoli colony via Vivarium — never shown in a whole-cell model — opening an antibiotic-potency prediction.

A whole-cell model is a black box you can now question — and every answer tells you not only what the cell does, but where to build the model next.
